Egypt's Mediterranean Coast

Egypt's Mediterranean coast offers a unique blend of ancient history and modern seaside charm. From the cosmopolitan port city of Alexandria with its Greco-Roman heritage to the exclusive beach resorts of the North Coast, this region provides a refreshing alternative to the desert landscapes. The Mediterranean climate, cultural diversity, and stunning coastline make it a perfect destination for history enthusiasts, beach lovers, and food connoisseurs.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

April to June & September to October - Ideal temperatures (20-28¬∞C), pleasant sea breezes, perfect for beach activities and sightseeing.

July to August - Warmest months (28-32¬∞C), peak tourist season, vibrant atmosphere but crowded beaches.

November to March - Cooler temperatures (15-20¬∞C), occasional rain, fewer crowds, great for cultural activities.

Getting Around

Transportation: Alexandria has extensive public transport, taxis, and ride-hailing services. Many attractions are walkable in the city center.

North Coast: Best accessed by private car or organized tours. Limited public transport to remote resort areas.

Parking: Available but can be challenging in summer. Resort areas offer ample parking facilities.

What to Pack

Beach Wear: Swimwear, cover-ups, sun hat, sunglasses, high-SPF sunscreen.

Evening Wear: Light jacket or sweater for sea breezes, smart casual for dining.

Sightseeing: Comfortable walking shoes, modest attire for religious sites, camera.

Essentials: Reusable water bottle, power bank, first aid kit, insect repellent.
